Hello, we are EnFold, a charity providing services to the Autistic Community in Northamptonshire, UK.

Our services are aimed at improving the lives of those living with Autism Spectrum Condition and their families, whether diagnosed or undiagnosed, adult or child, by removing barriers and increasing understanding.

We provide help and advice through our 1:1 support service, our groups and our Peer support service, and give Autistic people a voice to shape services. We also educate the wider community to break down barriers.

Find out more about our services

How do I get support before or after an Autism diagnosis?

We recognise that going through the diagnostic pathway is a long and challenging process resulting in questions as well as answers.‍
We offer 4-6 sessions of support, in the home or virtually, offering practical support and service signposting tailored to the individual and family’s needs.

Find out more about our Autism Support Service

How can my organisation learn more about Autism from people with lived experience?

Aimed at services, organisations and businesses, this consists of 4 hrs of quality training in understanding Autism and its impact on those working with Autistic individuals. A chance to reflect on practice and consider strategies and approaches to best support individuals living with Autism. Developed and delivered by experts with lived experience.

Find out more about our Training Service

What support is available for Autistic young people at risk from mental health admission?

This service is supporting young people under 25 with Autism and/or Learning Disability who are at risk of hospitalisation due to their mental health.  This project sits within the national Keyworker programme with referrals coming directly from the Dynamic Support Register (DSR).

Find out more about our Peer Support Service

How can young Autistic people have a voice in shaping support services in a social setting?

If you are aged between 11 and 25 and would like to be involved in having a say in the development of services in Northamptonshire for Autistic young people, as well as meeting other young people with ASC, then this is the group for you.

Find out more about our YAAP Groups

How can I socialise with other Autistic adults in a safe space?

In response to need across the county we have established Adult Social Groups in Kettering, Northampton, Wellingborough and Rushden  that offer social connection and interaction, personal development and of course fun!

Find out more about our Adult Support Groups

Where can I meet parents of other Austistic young people and enjoy whole family social activities?

We offer a social support group for parents of children with, or seeking diagnosis of, High Functioning Autism. We run subsidised days out for the whole family, organise speakers and parent meets in the Northants area. Click below to join our Facebook Page

Find out more about our Parent Support Groups
