What we’ve been up to…

Commonkind is a registered charity and Public Benevolent Institution (PBI) dedicated to providing benevolent relief through community service. While our objective is to support those in need, our delivery model is unique: we empower children and young people to be the drivers of that care.

We are incredibly grateful that our model resonated with Brisbane Airport's 2026 Community Giving Fund and that we have been selected as one of the lucky recipients of a 2026 grant.

Brisbane Airport's generous support bolsters our Learn By Heart program, allowing us to continue to deliver benevolent relief to Queenslanders in need today, and scale our service pathways to empower children and young people to become compassionate community leaders for tomorrow.

Thank you, Brisbane Airport, for helping us pave the way so that children and young people can lead the way to a kinder and more connected Australia.

Intergenerational practice is often viewed as a "nice to have" activity, but for the aged care and education sectors, it is a powerful tool for systemic change.

Today for Global Intergenerational Week, we are highlighting how these connections transform service delivery. When schools and aged care providers collaborate, we see:
Improved cognitive and social outcomes for students.
A significant reduction in social isolation for seniors.

At Commonkind, we facilitate these "Heart to Heart" connections to support our mission to build a kinder and more connected Australia.

Whether it is a student sharing a story with a resident or a shared community project, these moments create positive impact for everyone involved.

We invite leaders particularly in the education and aged care sectors to watch our video series, to see how these programs can be integrated for lasting impact.

This month's Village Voice Brisbane featured one of our recent Learn By Heart sessions at All Hallows'​ School. One thing became very clear during the session: young people don't just want to help - they want to lead.

In a single morning of "joyful mayhem," Year 6 students packed and sealed 455 breakfast bowls for commonkind. These aren't just meals; they are a direct line of support to Queensland students who would otherwise start their school day on an empty stomach.

As our CEO, Carolina Puleston, noted during the session, it is the energy and enthusiasm of these students that makes this environment so motivating.

This is the "Learn by Heart" model in action. By involving students in the physical work of service - packing, sealing, and donating - we aren't just solving a problem for today; we’re building a habit of compassion for tomorrow.

A huge thank you to Maeve, Rosie, Ellie, Maya, and the entire Year 6 cohort for showing us how it's done.

We acknowledge and pay respect to the past, present and future Traditional Custodians and Elders of the lands where commonkind serves and extend that respect to all Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islanders. We recognise the world’s oldest living culture and the strong sense of community among Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples and the power of this connection not only for the individual but also for society. We aspire to strengthen the communities in which we live, work, study and play. In our mission to build a kinder community, we are inspired by the rich history of kindness to people and planet demonstrated by First Nations communities across Australia.
