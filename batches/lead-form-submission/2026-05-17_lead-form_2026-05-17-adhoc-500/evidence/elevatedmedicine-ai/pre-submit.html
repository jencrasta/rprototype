Beyond prevention

Longevity Medicine

Longevity medicine is conventional medicine extended from reactive to proactive, from waiting for disease to measuring what's happening inside your body before disease develops.

It integrates lifestyle medicine adjustments, advanced screening, and evidence-based geroscience with conventional medicine, treating the person as a whole rather than treating isolated symptoms or diseases. Or, as a colleague recently put it: it's simply good medicine.

About

The doctor
Behind The Words.

The purpose was always the same prevent the disease that's preventable, and know how to treat the unpreventable. We are taught some of it at medical school and continue now in residency. But mainly what to do when things have already started to go wrong.

The questions remained. What can we do to increase the chances of staying healthy in the first place and keep people out of medications and hospitals? And if there is a symptom, am I doing everything possible to find an answer? Those questions led me to lifestyle medicine, and from there further to longevity medicine.

And AI gives us tools to push the limits of what medicine can offer.

This page is where I write, in plain language, the things I have learned and am still learning and would like to spend time explaining to every single patient or person. Information is reviewed and updated as evidence develops, guidelines change, and my own clinical thinking deepens.

I write from a desk that always has too many open tabs, often at hours when a doctor with longevity awareness really shouldn't be awake. My own imperfect life keeps me honest about the difference between knowing what's healthy and actually living it.

— K.
