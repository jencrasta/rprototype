About the Festival

Yeah, we're doing this.

The Canadian Independent Art Film Festival is exactly what it sounds like — a festival for indie films that actually have something to say. No blockbuster money, no studio polish. Just filmmakers who gave a damn and made something real.

We're running this June 19th at the Morpheus Clinic of Hypnosis — because honestly, what better place to screen art films than somewhere that messes with your perception of reality? It fits perfectly.

This is a space for short films, experimental work, documentaries, personal projects — anything that pushes against the mainstream. Canadian directors, Canadian stories, Canadian voices. That's the whole point.

We keep the vibe tight, the audience engaged, and the conversations flowing after the screenings. Expect drinks, debate, and a genuine celebration of the weird and wonderful.

Submission Guidelines

The Rules (keep it simple)

We tried to keep this as painless as possible. Here's what you need to know before submitting.

✅ Who Can Submit

  • Films must be directed or co-directed by a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
  • Films produced (at least partially) in Canada are also welcome — if there's a genuine Canadian creative connection, reach out and we'll figure it out
  • Both emerging and established filmmakers are encouraged to apply — seriously, we mean it
  • Student films, passion projects, professional productions — all fine

📽️ What Kind of Films

  • Short films: up to 30 minutes
  • Feature films: 60 minutes and over (limited slots, contact us first)
  • Experimental, documentary, narrative, animation — all categories welcome
  • Films in any language, but must include English subtitles if not in English or French
  • Films completed after January 1, 2023 are eligible

📋 Submission Requirements

  • Submit via FilmFreeway or the form below
  • Include a working screener link (password-protected is fine — just give us the password)
  • Director's bio and a short synopsis (no more than 200 words)
  • Film poster or still images (min. 1080px wide)
  • Technical specs: resolution, runtime, aspect ratio

🚫 What We Don't Accept

  • Films with no Canadian connection whatsoever
  • Content that promotes hate or discrimination — hard no
  • Previously screened at the same festival (new films only)
  • Unfinished rough cuts — at least a picture lock, please
💬
Got a special case? If your film doesn't fit neatly into any of these boxes but you think it belongs here, just email us. We're real people and we actually read it: info@canadianartsindie.org
