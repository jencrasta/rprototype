Sacramento Energy Rebates and Tax Credit Information for 2026

Upgrading your home can still come with meaningful savings. Many Sacramento-area homeowners may qualify for active local rebates on heat pump HVAC systems, heat pump water heaters, insulation, and other efficiency improvements. You can review the current SMUD rebate program, active Golden State Rebates offers, and local utility rebate pages for Roseville Electric and Lodi Electric.

For federal guidance, visit the official IRS home energy tax credit page and the easy-to-read ENERGY STAR tax credit guide. For income-qualified California incentives, the current HEEHRA / TECH rebate status shows that single-family funding is fully reserved statewide and new requests are currently being waitlisted.

At American Home Energy Savers, we help homeowners identify which rebates may apply before work begins so you can make a smart decision with clear pricing, better comfort, and fewer surprises. Let us schedule a no cost, no obligation inspection to help you determine what programs would best help you and your family.

Attention Sacramento Area Community Members

About half of the water we use at home is spent outdoors on landscaping. Californians have made great strides to improve indoor water efficiency; however, outdoor water efficiency achieves much more water savings. We each can use up to 82 gallons of water a day at home. We can all use at least 20 percent less water by installing water-efficient fixtures and appliances. The average American family spends more than $1,000 per year in water costs, but can save more than $380 annually from retrofitting with WaterSense labeled fixtures and ENERGY STAR certified appliances.
